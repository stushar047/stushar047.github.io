About

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Engineering at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. My research focuses on neuromorphic computing, spiking neural networks, and memristive in-memory computing systems.

My work bridges device-level modeling, mixed-signal circuit design, and hardware-aware learning algorithms to enable energy-efficient, biologically inspired AI systems for vision, continual learning, and edge intelligence.

Google Scholar | GitHub | LinkedIn

Research Interests

  • Neuromorphic computing with memristive hardware
  • Spiking neural networks and in-memory dot-product engines
  • Hardware-aware learning and energy-efficient AI accelerators
  • Reservoir computing and sleep-inspired continual learning
  • Computer vision and edge AI systems

Graduate Research Assistant — SENECA Design Lab

University of Tennessee, Knoxville (2022 – Present)

  • Contributed to neuromorphic chip tape-out using HfO₂-based memristive devices fabricated in IBM 65 nm technology, including WTA circuits and dot-product engines.
  • Developed in-memory template matching and motion recognition systems achieving up to 678× improvement in power–delay product.
  • Created NeuroPAT, a neuromorphic energy estimation framework integrated with the RAVENS platform.
  • Investigated STDP, reservoir computing, and sleep-inspired replay for continual learning and reduced catastrophic forgetting.
