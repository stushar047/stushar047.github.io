Graduate Research Assistant — SENECA Design Lab

University of Tennessee, Knoxville (2022 – Present)

  • Contributed to neuromorphic chip tape-out using HfO₂-based memristive devices fabricated in IBM 65 nm technology, including WTA circuits and dot-product engines.
  • Developed in-memory template matching and motion recognition systems achieving up to 678× improvement in power–delay product.
  • Created NeuroPAT, a neuromorphic energy estimation framework integrated with the RAVENS platform.
  • Investigated STDP, reservoir computing, and sleep-inspired replay for continual learning and reduced catastrophic forgetting.
